description Campaign Story

For the past five years, the eastern region of Kenya has experienced unprecedented drought conditions. The three villages of Makueni, Kitui, and Machakos have seen their primary water sources dry up completely, forcing residents—primarily women and children—to walk over 15 kilometers daily just to find water that is often unsafe for consumption.

This initiative aims to install three heavy-duty, solar-powered atmospheric water generators and purification systems, one in each village center. These systems are capable of extracting moisture directly from the air and purifying local contaminated sources, providing up to 5,000 liters of clean drinking water per day without relying on grid electricity or diesel generators.

Budget Breakdown

  • Equipment ($35,000): 3x Solar Atmospheric Generators & Filtration Units.
  • Logistics ($8,000): Shipping, customs, and secure transport to remote areas.
  • Installation & Training ($5,000): Hiring local engineers and training a community maintenance committee.
  • Contingency ($2,000): Unexpected delays or material costs.

By fully funding this project, we can eliminate the daily struggle for water, drastically reduce waterborne diseases, and allow children to return to school instead of spending their days fetching water.
